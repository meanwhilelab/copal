# Copal — brand guidelines

## Core idea

Copal is resin before it becomes amber: it can preserve what it holds without becoming inert. The identity expresses **protected potential** — a warm living inclusion held inside a compact structure, with a narrow opening toward what follows.

## Logo

The mark has three inseparable components:

1. the charcoal outer form: custody, structure, reliability;
2. the clear interval: context, discernment, room to think;
3. the resin inclusion: living material that can still change.

Use the horizontal lockup by default. Use the standalone mark for avatars, favicons, application icons and very small placements. Use the stacked lockup when the composition is approximately square.

### Clear space

Keep clear space equal to **one quarter of the mark’s width** on every side. Do not place the logo against detailed imagery.

### Minimum size

- Standalone mark: 16 px digital, 5 mm print.
- Horizontal lockup: 120 px digital, 32 mm print.
- Below 24 px, use the favicon version, whose surrounding tile protects the silhouette.

### Do not

- rotate, stretch or mirror the mark;
- recolor the resin inclusion independently of the approved palette;
- close the opening or remove the clear interval;
- add gradients, shadows, outlines or glass effects;
- use the gold as body text on the bone background;
- pair the mark with another typeface inside the lockup.

## Color

| Token | Hex | Role |
|---|---:|---|
| Charcoal | `#3F4043` | Primary text, structure, dark fields |
| Resin | `#D98924` | Inclusion, active accents, selected states |
| Bone | `#FBF5F1` | Primary background |
| Deep resin | `#8F5219` | Accessible warm text/accent |
| Pollen | `#E7B34E` | Soft highlights and diagrams |
| Ash | `#6F6A61` | Secondary text |

Charcoal on Bone is the principal contrast pair. Resin is an accent, not a text color. For small text on Bone use Charcoal, Ash or Deep resin.

## Typography

### Display and brand voice — Newsreader

Use Newsreader Regular for titles, statements and editorial moments. It gives Copal a literary quality without making it nostalgic. Use sentence case; avoid all-caps headlines.

### Interface and long text — Inter

Use Inter for product UI, documentation, code-adjacent labels and body copy. Recommended weights: 400, 500 and 600.

Both families are open source. The SVG lockups include sensible system fallbacks but production environments should load the named fonts.

## Voice

Copal speaks like a careful collaborator:

- **calm:** no urgency theatre;
- **specific:** name the object, decision or next step;
- **situated:** preserve provenance and uncertainty;
- **generative:** end with what can happen next;
- **non-mystical:** intelligence is visible in structure, not claims of magic.

Prefer: “Here is where the thinking stopped.”  
Avoid: “Your second brain remembers everything.”

Prefer: “Three related threads may be worth reopening.”  
Avoid: “AI has discovered hidden insights.”

## Imagery and graphic language

Use close, tactile images of paper, resin, wood, bindings, marginalia and working surfaces — never literal amber gemstones or generic AI imagery. Graphic compositions should feel accumulated rather than tiled: overlapping notes, restrained strata, a single warm inclusion inside a neutral field.

Use generous negative space. One warm focal point is stronger than many gold accents.

## Relationship to Meanwhile

Copal may be described as **“an open-source project by meanwhile”**, but the Meanwhile name should not be fused into the master logo. Keep the relationship editorial and subordinate so Copal can stand independently as an open-source project.
